Expression Notes

MISSION 1
ANŌ
Excuse me... / Um...
あのう
Expression Explanation

“ANŌ” is a soft cushion word used before speaking to someone.

It helps your speech sound more polite and less direct.

It is often used:
  • before asking questions
  • before interrupting someone
  • before talking to strangers
相手に話しかける前に使うクッション言葉です。
失礼に聞こえにくくします。
When should you use “ANŌ”?
MISSION 2
SŌ DESU KA ⤴
Really?
そうですか？
SŌ DESU KA ⤵
I see.
そうですか
Expression Explanation

Japanese changes meaning through intonation.

Rising intonation (⤴) means surprise or confirmation.

Falling intonation (⤵) means understanding.
イントネーションで意味が変わります。
上げると驚き、下げると納得になります。
Which one means “I see”?
MISSION 3
KYŌSHI
Teacher
きょうし
Expression Explanation

“SENSEE” is an honorable title.

Using it for yourself may sound arrogant.

When introducing your own profession, “KYŌSHI” sounds more natural and humble.
「せんせい」は敬称です。
自分に使うと少し偉そうに聞こえる場合があります。
自分の職業紹介では「きょうし」が自然です。
Which expression is more natural?
MISSION 4
NI-MAN EN
20,000 yen
にまんえん
Expression Explanation

Japanese counts large numbers differently from English.

English groups numbers by 1,000.

Japanese groups numbers by 10,000.

10,000 = MAN
英語は1000単位で区切ります。
日本語は10000単位（まん）で区切ります。
20,000 yen is...
MISSION 5
ONEGAI SHIMASU
Please...
おねがいします
Expression Explanation

“KUDASAI” is used for requesting concrete items.

“ONEGAI SHIMASU” sounds more polite and more natural in restaurants.

It can also be used for services and abstract requests.
「ください」は具体的な物に使います。
「おねがいします」は少し丁寧で、レストランで自然です。
Which expression sounds more polite?
